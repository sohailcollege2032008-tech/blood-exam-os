Q26 Written

Write 4 functions of complement.

Biological functions — 4:
1)Opsonization (C3b) — enhances macrophage + neutrophil phagocytic engulfment.
2)Inflammatory mediation (C3a, C4a, C5a) — triggers mast cell degranulation +
attracts neutrophils.
3)Direct cytolysis (C5b-9 MAC) — punches cylindrical holes in
bacterial/foreign membranes.
4)Regulatory clearance — facilitates immune complex removal + regulates
continued activation.

Q28 Written

Describe the mechanism of type II hypersensitivity reactions.

Initiation phase — 1:
1)Immunoglobulin binding — preformed IgG or IgM antibodies bind directly to
cell-surface antigens.
Effector destruction mechanisms — 3:
1)Complement-mediated lysis — activates classical pathway → MAC formation →
direct cell destruction.
2)Opsonization + Phagocytosis — antibody/complement coating → enhances
macrophage engulfment.
3)Antibody-Dependent Cellular Cytotoxicity (ADCC) — recruits NK cells +
neutrophils → targeted cell lysis.
Q29 Written

Write 4 methods for prevention of graft rejection.

Pre-transplant donor selection — 3:
1)Blood grouping — matches donor/recipient ABO and Rh compatibility.
2)Tissue (HLA) typing — determines closest major histocompatibility complex
(MHC) match.
3)Cross-matching — screens recipient serum for pre-formed antibodies against
donor tissue.
Post-transplant medical therapies — 2:
1)General immunosuppression — administers Cyclosporine, Corticosteroids,
Azathioprine, or OKT3.
2)Antigen-specific tolerance — administers low-dose donor antigens to induce
specific immune unresponsiveness.

Q61 Written

Describe the structural feature of hemoglobin Al molecule.

Protein (Globin) subunits — 2:
1)Alpha ($\alpha$) chains — 2 polypeptide chains — 141 amino acids each
2)Beta ($\beta$) chains — 2 polypeptide chains — 146 amino acids each

Prosthetic (Heme) groups — 2:
1)Protoporphyrin IX rings — 4 organic molecules — structural scaffold
2)Ferrous iron ($Fe^{2+}$) atoms — 4 central atoms — reversible $O_2$ binding

Overall quaternary structure — 2:
1)Tetrameric assembly — $\alpha_1\beta_1$ and $\alpha_2\beta_2$ dimers — held
by non-covalent bonds
2)Central cavity — binds 2,3-BPG — regulates oxygen affinity
